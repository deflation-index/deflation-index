The Thesis

Technology creates exponential deflation through cost reductions in computing, communications, energy, and transportation. From 1990-2024, costs collapsed 84.3% across these sectors.

Yet consumer prices rose 155% during the same period. The divergence is stark: technology pushed prices down 5.2% annually, while monetary expansion (M2 +5.9% annually) pushed them up. Official CPI showed only +2.7% inflation—masking an annual gap of 7.8 percentage points between tech's deflationary force and reported inflation.

This reveals a powerful systematic divergence. Monetary policy is a counterforce to technology's productivity gains. Technology drives costs down, monetary expansion drives them up. The mathematics: technological gains reduce prices, monetary policy prevents those reductions from reaching consumers. The question becomes: where does the productivity go?

What Actually Happened

The Abundance Gap: 545 Percentage Points

Technology delivered 84% deflation ↓. Money supply expanded 615% ↑. These are the two systematic forces—one pushing prices down, one pushing them up. Combined with CPI rising 155%, the net gap: 545 percentage points of separation.

Consumer prices rose 155%—absorbing most of the technological deflation. The abundance went into the system, but not into lower prices for consumers.

35 Years of Deflation

1990

Base Year

Computing costs astronomical. Mobile phones rare and expensive. Solar power niche. Base year for all calculations. M2: $3.0T.

2000

Dot-Com Boom

Internet adoption accelerates. Computing costs collapse 95% from 1990. Communications revolution begins. M2: $4.9T (+48%).

2007

Smartphone Era

iPhone launches. Mobile computing becomes universal. Storage costs approach zero. Communications essentially free. M2: $7.4T (+124%).

2010

Energy Inflection

Solar costs begin exponential decline. Battery technology accelerates. LED adoption reaches scale. Clean energy deflation begins. M2: $8.8T (+167%).

2020

COVID Money Printing

M2 expands 24% in single year—largest peacetime expansion in history. Tech deflation accelerates. Gap widens dramatically. M2: $18.4T (+457%).

2024

Current State

Computing 99.99%+ cheaper than 1990. Communications essentially free. Solar cheapest energy in history. Purchasing power down 60%. M2: $21.3T (+615%).
