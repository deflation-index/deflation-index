## KEY INSIGHTS

**1. Technology Delivers Exponential Cost Reduction**
- Computing: 10x cheaper every 7.7 years
- Energy: Accelerating post-2010 (learning curves)
- Cross-sector convergence amplifying effects

**2. Quality-Adjusted Deflation Even Higher**
- Raw prices understate true gains by 30-50%
- Performance improvements: 15-25x across sectors
- Reliability, efficiency, convenience all improving

**3. The Wealth Transfer**
- Technology should have cut prices 84%
- M2 growth should have raised prices 600%
- Net potential: +516% price rise
- Actual: +150%
- **Missing: ~370 percentage points**

**4. Where the Gains Went**
- Corporate profits (+300% since 1990)
- Asset prices (stocks +1,500%, housing +400%)
- Complexity costs (healthcare, education, housing)
- Quality improvements (not captured in CPI)

## THE STORY IN THREE SENTENCES

1. **Technology made everything dramatically cheaper** – 84% deflation over 35 years across computing, communications, energy, and transportation.

2. **Money supply expanded massively** – M2 grew 600%, but consumer prices only rose 150%, creating a 370 percentage point gap.

3. **Someone captured the difference** – Corporate profits, asset inflation, and complexity costs absorbed the productivity gains that should have gone to consumers.
